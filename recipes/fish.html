Bengali Fried Rohu Fish

Description

A delicious and easy fish fry recipe, considered a household staple amongst Bengalis! Also called rui, but in English-speaking countries it'd do you well to just ask for carp :-)

A dish that can also be eaten for lunch, dinner or maybe (if you're brave enough) breakfast too, not too spicy nor too boring; I do see the vision, you'll have to keep the windows closed for a while though.

Ingredients

  • 400 GM fish fillet
  • 2 tbsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp gram powder
  • 1 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • 1/2 tsp coriander powder
  • Oil for frying

Steps

  1. Wash the fish thoroughly.
  2. In a big bowl, add all the spices together.
  3. Add in the fish, and coat it thoroughly with the spices.
  4. Let it marinate for about 30 minutes.
  5. Heat up the oil on a frying pan until high temperature, or until you feel its heat.
  6. Slowly add in the fish pieces one-by-one.
  7. Let it cook and build a solid crisp cover on one side, and then turn it around and do the same.
  8. Take it out and place it on a kitchen towel or a bunch of tissue papers to soak up the oil.
  9. Garnish with onion and lemon slices if needed.
  10. Serve and enjoy!
